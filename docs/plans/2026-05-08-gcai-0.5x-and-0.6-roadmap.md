# GreenChemistry.ai 0.5.x and 0.6 Roadmap

> **For Hermes:** Use this document to keep backlog grooming honest: 0.5.x finishes the equivalent-chemistry thesis; 0.6 introduces process redesign and should not be backported casually.

**Goal:** Clarify the near-term product boundary for GreenChemistry.ai and separate current-wedge hardening from the heavier redesign implied by literature on greener process architecture.

**Architecture:** Version 0.5.x remains a hybrid deterministic + LLM-assisted protocol analyzer focused on greener equivalent chemistry: substitutions, evidence, scoring, and before/after impact. Version 0.6 expands scope from molecule-level and condition-level optimization to route-level redesign: one-pot/telescoped alternatives, transfer/purification burden, and literature-backed procedural precedent.

**Tech Stack:** Next.js app, Python chemistry service, PubChem/CHEM21/GHS evidence, optional local-model backends, future vector retrieval.

---

## Product Boundary

### 0.5.x — Equivalent chemistry hardening

The product promise in 0.5.x:

> Give me a protocol and I will identify greener equivalent choices, explain why, and show the projected impact with evidence.

This includes:
- substitution quality
- deterministic scoring coverage across the 12 principles
- evidence grounding and citations
- before/after rescoring
- validation against known protocols
- literature retrieval that supports recommendations and validation

This does **not** include:
- route architecture redesign
- one-pot / telescoped synthesis proposals as a primary workflow
- claims about experimentally validated process improvements beyond cited precedent
- strong claims about reducing transfer burden, purification burden, or operator complexity unless those metrics are explicitly modeled

### 0.6 — Equivalent process redesign

The product promise in 0.6:

> Give me a protocol and I will identify not only greener equivalent chemicals, but also greener equivalent process structures.

This may include:
- one-pot and telescoped alternatives
- transfer-count reduction
- purification-burden reduction
- shorter runtime / fewer heating phases
- route-level precedent retrieval from the literature
- route-architecture comparison UX

This requires a deliberate redesign because it changes:
- what the system proposes
- what evidence is needed
- what can be claimed safely
- what scoring dimensions are first-class

---

## Why this split exists

Published green chemistry papers often prove value through:
- experimentally validated route changes
- reduced material throughput
- reduced solvent use
- simpler workflow
- reduced cleanup burden
- shorter runtime
- improved reproducibility

Those are valuable signals for the future product, but they are not the same as the current 0.5.x thesis. If they are pulled into the current backlog without boundary control, the roadmap blurs and the product starts overpromising process optimization before the data model and evaluation framework exist.

---

## 0.5.x success criteria

0.5.x is ready to close when GreenChemistry.ai can reliably do all of the following:
- parse a protocol into usable structure
- score the protocol across the 12 principles with explicit confidence labels
- produce greener equivalent substitutions that are chemically credible
- cite the evidence behind major flags and replacements
- show projected before/after score deltas after accepted changes
- validate performance against a small benchmark set of known greener alternatives and known problematic protocols

## 0.6 entry criteria

Do not begin 0.6 implementation until:
- P1/P5/P6 and before/after rescoring are trustworthy enough to serve as a baseline
- evidence retrieval is good enough to support route-level precedent lookup
- validation infrastructure exists for separating substitution quality from redesign quality
- product/claim boundaries are explicitly documented

---

## Suggested implementation order

### Finish 0.5.x first
1. Deterministic scoring quality
2. Evidence grounding quality
3. Before/after rescoring quality
4. Retrieval support
5. Validation benchmark set
6. UX clarity around confidence and score deltas

### Then design 0.6
1. Process-complexity scoring model
2. Purification/cleanup burden model
3. Route-level literature retrieval
4. Route-comparison UX
5. Benchmark set for published process redesigns
